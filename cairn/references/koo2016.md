# koo2016 — ICC selection, reporting, and the interpretation bands

**Citation.** Koo TK, Li MY (2016). "A Guideline of Selecting and Reporting
Intraclass Correlation Coefficients for Reliability Research." *Journal of
Chiropractic Medicine* 15, 155–163. DOI 10.1016/j.jcm.2016.02.012.
PDF: `cairn/references/sources/koo2016.pdf` (gitignored). Printed exactly as
"Journal of Chiropractic Medicine (2016) 15, 155–163", DOI as
`http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.jcm.2016.02.012`; **no issue number appears**
anywhere in the article (the "15(2)" in `BIBLIOGRAPHY.md` is not from the paper).
Series header, p. 155: "Cracking the Code: Providing Insight Into the
Fundamentals of Research and Evidence-Based Practice".

**Role.** The package's primary **interpretation-band** source and the source of
the "judge the interval, not the point" caveat in `vignettes/getting-started.Rmd`.
No oracle values trace here (nothing in `ORACLES.md` cites it) — this is a
guidance/prose source only, and it is the source IP3 is most sensitive to.

## The interpretation bands — exact printed wording

The bands appear **four times**, with the same wording each time: Abstract
(p. 155), § "ICC Interpretation" (p. 158), the boxed guideline in § "How to
Interpret ICC in Published Studies" (p. 161), and the Fig 3 caption (p. 162).

Boxed guideline, p. 161, verbatim: "Values less than 0.5 are indicative of poor
reliability, values between 0.5 and 0.75 indicate moderate reliability, values
between 0.75 and 0.9 indicate good reliability, and values greater than 0.90
indicate excellent reliability."

Exact inequality directions **as printed**:

| Band | Printed form | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| poor | "less than 0.5" | strict `<` |
| moderate | "between 0.5 and 0.75" | "between" — inclusivity **not stated** |
| good | "between 0.75 and 0.9" | "between" — inclusivity **not stated** |
| excellent | "greater than 0.90" | strict `>` |

So `< 0.5` and `> 0.90` are unambiguous; **0.5, 0.75 and 0.9 are left ambiguous**
by "between", and the paper never disambiguates them. The bands exist only in
prose (plus the Fig 3 caption) — there is no band *table* to contradict, so the
ambiguity is internal to the wording. Note the paper writes the upper cut as
"0.9" for "good" and "0.90" for "excellent" (pp. 155, 158, 161, 162): read
strictly, an ICC of exactly 0.90 falls in no band. p. 158 also attaches a scope
condition — the bands assume "at least 30 heterogeneous samples" and "at least
3 raters".

The paper opens the same section by disclaiming universality: "there are no
standard values for acceptable reliability using ICC" (p. 158), noting a low ICC
may reflect low subject variability or few raters rather than poor agreement.

## Judge against the 95% CI, not the point estimate (the load-bearing item)

§ "How to Interpret ICC in Published Studies", p. 161, verbatim: "the 95%
confident interval of the ICC estimate (not the ICC estimate itself) should be
used as the basis to evaluate the level of reliability" — followed immediately by
the boxed band guideline above. (The paper writes "confident interval" for
"confidence interval" throughout, pp. 159, 161, 162.)

Stated rationale, p. 158: "the ICC estimate obtained from a reliability study is
only an expected value of the true ICC", so it is "logical" to test whether the
obtained value significantly exceeds a suggested cut by statistical inference.
Restated in the Conclusion, p. 162: it is "more appropriate to evaluate the level
of reliability based on the 95% confident interval of the ICC estimate, not the
ICC estimate itself."

Worked applications of the rule (p. 161): a 95% CI of 0.83–0.94 is read as "good"
to "excellent"; a CI of 0.92–0.99 is "excellent" "because even in the worst case
scenario, the true ICC is still greater than 0.9". Fig 3 (p. 162) is the reader's
flowchart: complete ICC-form information reported? → correct form used? → only
then interpret using the CI as the basis; otherwise "Interpret ICC with caution!"

## Selection guidance

- **10 forms** after McGraw & Wong (1996), crossed from "Model" (1-way random,
  2-way random, 2-way fixed), "Type" (single rater/measurement vs. mean of *k*),
  and "Definition" (consistency vs. absolute agreement) — p. 156.
- **Four questions** driving the choice (p. 156): (1) same set of raters for all
  subjects? (2) raters randomly sampled vs. a specific sample? (3) single rater
  or the mean of multiple? (4) consistency or agreement? Q1–Q2 → Model, Q3 →
  Type, Q4 → Definition.
- **Model** (pp. 156–157): one-way random when different raters rate different
  subjects (called rare in practice; the multicenter case is the exception);
  two-way random when raters are sampled and results generalize to like raters;
  two-way mixed when "the selected raters are the only raters of interest",
  results not generalizable beyond them.
- **Type** (pp. 157–158): match the reliability design to the intended
  measurement protocol — mean-of-*k* if the application averages *k* raters,
  single-rater if a single rater's measurement is the basis, "even though the
  reliability experiment involves 2 or more raters" (p. 158).
- **Definition** (p. 158): absolute agreement asks whether `y` equals `x`;
  consistency asks whether `y = x + c` for a systematic offset `c`.
- **Test-retest / intrarater** (p. 158): two-way mixed model, and absolute
  agreement "should always be chosen" — repeated measurements cannot be regarded
  as randomized samples, and consistency would be meaningless there.
- **Table 3 (p. 157)** is the Shrout–Fleiss ↔ McGraw–Wong bridge: the six
  Shrout–Fleiss forms `ICC(1,1)`, `ICC(2,1)`, `ICC(3,1)`, `ICC(1,k)`, `ICC(2,k)`,
  `ICC(3,k)` mapped onto the 10 McGraw–Wong rows, with mean-square formulas; the
  four rows with no Shrout–Fleiss label are the two-way *random consistency* and
  two-way *mixed absolute-agreement* forms (single and mean). Note also that
  Table 3 gives the **same formula** to the two-way random and two-way mixed rows
  of a given definition/type — the paper says so explicitly (p. 158, fact 5): the
  difference "is not on the calculation but on the experimental design ... and
  the interpretation".
- **Fig 1 (p. 159)** is the researcher's selection flowchart implementing the
  four questions.

**ICC characteristics** (p. 158, five numbered facts): (1) identical data sets
give all ICCs = 1; (2) mean-of-*k* > corresponding single-rater; (3) absolute
agreement "generally gives a smaller ICC estimate" than consistency; (4) one-way
random generally gives a smaller estimate than the two-way models; (5) two-way
random and two-way mixed share a formula.

## Reporting recommendations (p. 160)

"There is currently a lack of standard for reporting ICC" — the suggested best
practice is to report: **software information**, the **"Model"**, **"Type"** and
**"Definition"** selections, and **both the ICC estimate and its 95% confidence
interval**. Example sentence given (p. 160): estimates and 95% intervals
"calculated using SPSS statistical package version 23 ... based on a mean-rating
(k = 3), absolute-agreement, 2-way mixed-effects model."

## Numerical values printed

- **Table 1 (p. 156)** — hypothetical L4-L5 flexion-extension ROM, 6 subjects;
  variance of measured 22.4°, true 9.6°, error 12.8°; reliability index =
  9.6/(9.6 + 12.8) = **0.43**.
- **Fig 2 (p. 160)** — three hypothetical 5-point data sets, Pearson R² = 1.00 in
  all three, each scored by all 10 forms. `y = x`: every ICC = 1.00. `y = x + 3`,
  single measurement, in the order one-way-random-absolute / two-way-random
  absolute / two-way-random consistency / two-way-mixed absolute / two-way-mixed
  consistency: 0.875 / 0.882 / 0.997 / 0.882 / 0.997 (mean measurement: 0.933 /
  0.938 / 0.999 / 0.938 / 0.999). `y = 2x`, same order: single 0.320 / 0.461 /
  0.787 / 0.461 / 0.787; mean 0.484 / 0.631 / 0.881 / 0.631 / 0.881.
- **Table 4 (p. 160)** — hypothetical SPSS output, single-rating
  absolute-agreement 2-way random effects, 3 raters × 30 subjects: single
  measures ICC **.932**, 95% CI **.879**–**.965**, F **45.606**, df1 **29**,
  df2 **58**, Sig **.000**. Read in text (p. 159) as "good" to "excellent" —
  the paper's own demonstration that the point estimate ("excellent") and the
  interval-based verdict differ.
- **Case description (p. 161)** — ultrasonography-based scoliosis measure, 35
  patients, 3 repeated measurements, single-measurement absolute-agreement 2-way
  mixed; reported ICC = **0.78**, 95% CI **0.72–0.84** → "moderate" to "good"
  (judged valid in § Case Discussion, p. 162).

## Open questions

- **IP3 vs. citing a benchmark table.** IP3 (`DESIGN.md`) is absolute: the
  package "never qualitatively labels ICC magnitude — no poor/good/excellent, no
  benchmark cutoffs in output, not even opt-in", but explicitly allows vignettes
  to "discuss why cutoffs are problematic, with citations". `getting-started.Rmd`
  reproduces this paper's bands as a *table*, not as computed output, and frames
  them as "conventions, not laws". That is inside IP3's letter. Worth a
  maintainer read anyway: the paper itself supplies the strongest anti-cutoff
  material (p. 158, "there are no standard values for acceptable reliability"),
  and the vignette currently leads with the table and caveats after. Raised as a
  framing question for the review gate, not a defect.
- **Table 3 formula erratum (p. 157, row 1).** The one-way random,
  absolute-agreement, single-rater formula is printed as
  `(MS_R − MS_W) / (MS_R + (k+1)MS_W)`. Every other single-rater row uses
  `(k−1)`, and `shrout1979.md` records `ICC(1,1) = (BMS − WMS)/(BMS + (k−1)WMS)`.
  Recorded as a finding in the source only — **no package value or oracle is
  touched**, and nothing in the repo cites this formula.
- **Model terminology is internally inconsistent.** p. 156 and the Table 3
  footnote a (p. 157) name McGraw & Wong's third model "2-way fixed effects",
  and the Fig 1 caption (p. 159) repeats "2-way fixed effects"; the body text,
  Table 3's row labels, and the Fig 1 boxes themselves all say "2-way mixed
  effects". The paper never states the two are the same thing.
- **Fig 1 dead-ends the one-way branch (p. 159).** Inspected at high
  magnification: the "One-way random effects" Model box has **no outgoing arrow**
  — the second "Intended measurement protocol?" diamond is fed only by the
  two-way random and two-way mixed boxes. So a reader who answers "No" to "same
  set of raters for all subjects?" gets no Type and no Definition selection from
  the flowchart, even though Table 3 lists one-way forms for both single and
  mean-of-*k*. (The Definition step would in any case be vacuous: Table 3 offers
  no one-way consistency form.)
- **"Confident interval" for "confidence interval"** on pp. 159, 161, 162,
  including inside the load-bearing p. 161 sentence quoted above. Quoted as
  printed; not corrected.
- **Issue number not printed.** `BIBLIOGRAPHY.md` and the vignette reference list
  cite "15(2)"; the article itself prints only volume 15 and the page range.
  Flagged for the maintainer — not changed here.
